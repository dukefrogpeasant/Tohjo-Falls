Splash pages


Splash pages are usually the first thing a visitor sees when they visit your site! They are usually a large image with some text on it, and a link to the main site which you enter by clicking on the image or text for if the image does not load.

To make a splash pic, you need an image editing program such as MS Paint or Photoshop. The size of a splash pic is usually about 600 pixels wide and 400 pixels tall, but you can make it any size you want. Just make sure it is not too big or too small. A good size is about 600x400 pixels.

When you make a splash pic, make sure it is related to your site subject. So don't make a mario splash pic when your sites about pokemon!

Splash pages can also be automatic in bringing you to the main site after a few seconds. This is done with a meta tag in the head section of your html code. Here is an example of a meta tag that will bring you to the main site after 5 seconds:

<meta http-equiv="refresh" content="5;url=home.html">

The "5" is the number of seconds to wait before going to the main site, and the "home.html" is the page to go to. Change the "5" to any number of seconds you want, and change the "home.html" to the page you want to go to. NEVER make it so that it redirects to index.html, as that will create an infinite loop since your splash page should be index.html.

also, probably dont make the count too short because then people wont see your splashpage.
